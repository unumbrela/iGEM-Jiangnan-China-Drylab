Evolution of Resistance

From the Golden Age of Antibiotics to the Resistance Crisis

1928
Discovery of Penicillin

Alexander Fleming discovered penicillin, launching the antibiotic era and saving millions of lives

1940s
Golden Age of Antibiotics

Streptomycin, chloramphenicol, tetracycline and other antibiotics discovered, dramatically reducing infectious disease mortality

1950s
First Resistance Reports

Penicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus emerged, signaling the beginning of bacterial counterattack

1980s
MRSA Outbreak

Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) spread widely in hospitals globally

2000s
Superbugs Emerge

NDM-1, KPC and other superbugs appeared, resistant to nearly all antibiotics

2025
AI-Powered Breakthrough

Jiangnan University iGEM team developed CytoFlow framework, bringing new hope for antimicrobial peptide design

Our Solution

Revolutionizing antimicrobial peptide design through AI technology to provide innovative solutions for the resistance crisis

Precise Problem Identification

Traditional antibiotics face resistance challenges, while natural antimicrobial peptide LL-37 has broad-spectrum activity but suffers from high cytotoxicity and poor stability, limiting clinical applications.

  • Identify traditional antibiotic limitations
  • Analyze LL-37 peptide defects
  • Define optimization design goals
  • Establish innovative research direction

AI-Driven Innovation

Developed CytoFlow framework integrating three models: CytoGrow for fermentation optimization, CytoGuard for activity prediction using protein language models and HyperGraph, and CytoEvolve combining Diffusion and RL.

  • CytoGrow - 25%+ biomass improvement
  • CytoGuard - 3 PLMs with Attention
  • CytoEvolve - Diffusion + RL
  • End-to-end optimization pipeline

Wet-Dry Lab Integration

Established complete DBTL cycle, combining AI design with yeast expression systems, forming continuous improvement through experimental validation and model optimization feedback.

  • Design - AI-driven peptide design
  • Build - Yeast expression construction
  • Test - Activity performance testing
  • Learn - Feedback model optimization

Breakthrough Results

Successfully achieved 25%+ yeast biomass improvement and designed improved LL-37 variants with comprehensive enhancements validated by wet lab, establishing solid foundation for clinical applications.

  • 25%+ biomass improvement
  • Comprehensive activity enhancement
  • Enhanced structural stability
  • Wet lab validated results
